# **Module 1: Mind, Culture, and the Foundations of Psychological Science**
## **16-Week Structure with Verified Textbook Readings**

**Document Version:** 2.0 (Bermúdez Removed)  
**Created:** November 2025  
**Purpose:** Complete Module 1 curriculum using verified, accessible textbook chapters

## **BLOCK 1: FOUNDATIONS (WEEKS 1-4)**

### **Week 1: Psychology as Science of the Human**

**Core Reading:**

- **Reisberg Ch. 1: "The Science of the Mind"** (~30 pages)
  - Covers: scope of cognitive psychology, methods, historical development
  - Establishes empirical foundations
  
- **James, W.** *Principles of Psychology* Vol. 1, Ch. 1 "The Scope of Psychology" (~40 pages)
  - Historical counterpoint to modern cognitive science
  - Available free: archive.org or Project Gutenberg

**Supplementary:**

- **Geertz, C.** "Thick Description" (from *Interpretation of Cultures*)
  - Anthropological perspective on human science

**Tasks:**

- **Essay (1,200 words):** "Is Psychology a Natural Science, a Human Science, or Both?"
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)
- Begin Research Logbook

**Assessment Focus:** Argument & Structure, Integration of Perspectives

---

### **Week 2: Behaviour, Cognition, and Meaning**

**Core Reading:**

- **Kim Ch. 3: "Mind and Behavior"** (~25 pages)
  - Philosophical behaviorism and its problems
  - Ryle's concept of mind
  
- **Skinner, B.F.** *Science and Human Behavior* (selections: Ch. 1-2) (~30 pages)
  - Radical behaviorism from the source

**Supplementary:**

- **Bateson, G.** *Steps to an Ecology of Mind* (Introduction)

**Alternative if Kim unavailable:**
- Stanford Encyclopedia: "Behaviorism" (https://plato.stanford.edu/entries/behaviorism/)

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 1 begins:** Design simple stimulus-response experiment
  - Use PsyToolkit or PsychoPy
  - Plan: What question? What stimuli? What measures?
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 3: Perception and Embodiment**

**Core Reading:**

- **Reisberg Ch. 3: "Visual Perception"** (~35 pages)
  - Empirical findings on perception
  - Bottom-up vs. top-down processing
  
- **Merleau-Ponty, M.** *Phenomenology of Perception* (Preface + Introduction) (~30 pages)
  - Phenomenological alternative to cognitive models

**Supplementary:**

- **Mauss, M.** "Techniques of the Body"
- **Gibson, J.J.** *The Ecological Approach to Visual Perception* (Ch. 1)

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 1 continues:** Collect data (minimum 5 participants)
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 4: Block 1 Integration & First Breaking Point**

**No new reading**

**Tasks:**

1. **Practical 1 due:** Full report (1,200 words)
   - Methods, results, analysis, interpretation
   - Connection to Week 2 readings on behavior/cognition
   
2. **Breaking Point Paper #1** (1,000 words): "Where This Framework Is Failing"
   - What has been difficult without human expertise?
   - Where has AI feedback been inadequate?
   - What questions remain unanswered?
   - What would change if you had peer discussion?
   - Decision: continue, modify, or pause?
   
3. **Block 1 Reflection** (500 words):
   - What did I learn in Weeks 1-3?
   - How confident am I in that learning?
   - What connections am I seeing between topics?

**Decision Point:** Proceed to Block 2, redesign, or pause?

---

## **BLOCK 2: MIND AND SUBJECTIVITY (WEEKS 5-8)**

### **Week 5: The Unconscious and Cultural Symbolism**

**Core Reading:**

- **Reisberg Ch. 15: "Conscious Thought, Unconscious Thought"** (~35 pages)
  - The cognitive unconscious
  - Subliminal processing, automaticity
  - Introspection's limits
  
- **Jung, C.G.** *Archetypes and the Collective Unconscious* (selections: "Concept of the Collective Unconscious") (~25 pages)

**Supplementary:**

- **Freud, S.** "The Unconscious"
- **Douglas, M.** *Purity and Danger* (Introduction)

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 2 begins:** Download perception dataset from OSF
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 6: Emotion, Reason, and Social Context**

**Core Reading:**

- **Damasio, A.** *Descartes' Error* Ch. 1-2 (~40 pages)
  - Somatic marker hypothesis
  - Emotion's role in rational decision-making
  
- **Kim Ch. 7: "Mental Causation"** (~30 pages)
  - How mental states cause behavior
  - Epiphenomenalism and its problems

**Supplementary:**

- **Nussbaum, M.** *Upheavals of Thought* (selections on emotion and judgment)
- **Lutz, C.** "Unnatural Emotions" (on cultural variation in emotion)

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 2 continues:** Analyze perception dataset
- **Random Reading Assignment:**
  - Go to *Journal of Consciousness Studies* or *Emotion Review*
  - Use random selection to pick one article from past 3 years
  - Read and write 500-word response connecting it to module themes
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 7: Self, Culture, and Symbolic Worlds**

**Core Reading:**

- **Vygotsky, L.** *Mind in Society* (Ch. 1-2: Tool and Symbol in Child Development) (~40 pages)
  - Cultural-historical development of mind
  - Internalization of cultural tools
  
- **Kim Ch. 1: "Introduction"** + **Ch. 2: "Mind as Immaterial Substance"** (~35 pages)
  - Overview of mind-body problem
  - Cartesian dualism and substance dualism

**Supplementary:**

- **Geertz, C.** "Person, Time and Conduct in Bali"
- **Ryle, G.** *The Concept of Mind* Ch. 1 (Descartes' Myth)

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 2 due:** Perception analysis report (1,000 words)
  - Analysis of dataset
  - Interpretation connecting to Merleau-Ponty and Reisberg
  - Reflection: What does empirical data reveal about embodiment claims?
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 8: Block 2 Integration & Second Breaking Point**

**No new reading**

**Tasks:**

1. **Essay (1,500 words):** "How Do Cultural Symbols Shape Unconscious Mind?"
   - Integrate readings from Weeks 5-7
   - Connect Jung, Douglas, Vygotsky, and Geertz
   
2. **Breaking Point Paper #2** (1,000 words): "The AI's Interpretive Limits"
   - Where has AI struggled to engage with phenomenological or symbolic material?
   - What kinds of understanding resist algorithmic feedback?
   - Has the framework become more or less useful since Block 1?
   
3. **Block 2 Reflection** (500 words)

**Decision Point:** Proceed to Block 3, modify, or pause?

---

## **BLOCK 3: EMBODIMENT AND REDUCTION (WEEKS 9-12)**

### **Week 9: Theories of Mind: Dualism to Physicalism**

**Core Reading:**

- **Kim Ch. 4: "Mind as the Brain"** (~30 pages)
  - Token vs. type physicalism
  - Psychoneural identity theory
  - Objections to identity theory
  
- **Churchland, P.** *Neurophilosophy* Part II: "Recent Developments in the Philosophy of Science" (selections: ~30 pages)
  - Read: Sections 1-3 of Part II
  - Reductive explanation in neuroscience
  - Co-evolution of theory

**Supplementary:**

- **Descartes, R.** *Meditations* (Meditation VI)
- **Kim, J.** "The Mind-Body Problem: Taking Stock After Forty Years" (article)

**Alternative if Churchland unavailable:**
- Stanford Encyclopedia: "Mind/Brain Identity Theory" 
- Plus Kim Ch. 10: "Reduction, Reductive Explanation, and Physicalism"

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 3 begins:** Emotion recognition task setup
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 10: Embodiment and Ecological Mind**

**Core Reading:**

- **Ingold, T.** *The Perception of the Environment* (selections on dwelling perspective) (~40 pages)
  - Recommended: Ch. 1 "Introduction" + Ch. 2 "Culture, Nature, Environment"
  
- **Varela, F., Thompson, E., Rosch, E.** *The Embodied Mind* (Introduction) (~20 pages)
  - Enactivism and embodied cognition
  - Critique of computational models

**Supplementary:**

- **Clark, A.** "Where Brain, Body and World Collide"

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 3 continues:** Collect emotion recognition data
- **Adversarial Essay Assignment Given:**
  - Topic: "Is Consciousness Reducible to Brain Processes?"
  - Due Week 12
  - Two versions required + meta-analysis
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words)

---

### **Week 11: Integration Reading Week**

**No new reading—catch up on supplementary materials or revisit difficult texts**

**Recommended revisiting:**
- Merleau-Ponty from Week 3
- Kim Ch. 4 from Week 9
- Reisberg Ch. 15 from Week 5

**Tasks:**

- **Practical 3 continues:** Analyze emotion data
- Work on adversarial essay (both versions)
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words): "What am I avoiding in my reading/thinking?"

---

### **Week 12: Block 3 Integration & Third Breaking Point**

**No new reading**

**Tasks:**

1. **Practical 3 due:** Emotion recognition report (1,000 words)
   - Methods, results, cultural interpretation (referencing Lutz)
   - Connection to Damasio's somatic marker hypothesis
   
2. **Adversarial Essay Due** (2,000 words total):
   - **Version A** (800 words): Your actual position on consciousness and reduction
   - **Version B** (800 words): Strongest possible contrary argument
   - **Meta-Analysis** (400 words): Which argument was stronger? What did arguing against yourself reveal?
   
3. **Breaking Point Paper #3** (1,000 words): "The Loneliness of Self-Directed Study"
   - What has been lost by studying alone?
   - Where would peer discussion have been valuable?
   - Has blog engagement (if any) mitigated isolation?
   - How has the relationship with AI evolved?

**Decision Point:** Proceed to Block 4 (synthesis), or pause before final push?

---

## **BLOCK 4: SYNTHESIS AND COMPLETION (WEEKS 13-16)**

### **Week 13: Consciousness and the Mind-Body Problem**

**Core Reading:**

- **Kim Ch. 9: "What Is Consciousness?"** (~35 pages)
  - Phenomenal consciousness vs. access consciousness
  - The explanatory gap
  - Qualia and their problems
  
- **Kim Ch. 11: "Consciousness and the Mind-Body Problem"** (~25 pages)
  - Consciousness as the hard problem for physicalism
  - Supervenience arguments

**Supplementary:**

- **Return to Reisberg Ch. 1**—read again with new understanding
- **Review your own Week 1 essay**—what has changed in your thinking?

**Tasks:**

- Begin **Concept Map** project
  - Use Obsidian, Gephi, or paper/pencil
  - Map all major thinkers and concepts from Module 1
  - Show connections, tensions, hierarchies
- Ethnographic reflection (300 words): "How has my thinking changed since Week 1?"

---

### **Week 14: Synthesis Seminar (Self-Directed)**

**No new reading**

**Tasks:**

- **Concept Map due** (visual + 800-word explanation)
  - How do empirical, philosophical, and anthropological approaches relate?
  - Where are productive tensions?
  - What remains unresolved?
- Begin drafting final integrative essay

---

### **Week 15: Final Integration**

**Tasks:**

- **Final Integrative Essay** (3,000 words): "How Can Anthropology and Philosophy Clarify the Aims of Psychological Science?"
  
  **Required elements:**
  - Engagement with at least 6 different weeks' readings
  - Integration of empirical findings from your practicals
  - Philosophical argument, not mere description
  - Anthropological awareness of cultural situatedness
  - Original synthesis
  
- Draft **Module Completion Reflection** (1,500 words):
  - What did I learn and how do I know I learned it?
  - What did AI contribute that I couldn't have achieved alone?
  - What did AI fail to provide?
  - How has this module changed my thinking about mind, culture, and science?

---

### **Week 16: Module Completion and Assessment**

**Tasks:**

1. **Final Essay due** (submit to AI for feedback and indicative grading)

2. **Module Completion Reflection due**

3. **Compile Failure Appendix:**
   - All instances of inadequate AI responses
   - Questions that remained unanswered
   - Feedback you ignored (rightly or wrongly)
   - Moments you wished for human dialogue
   
4. **External Touchstone:**
   - Identify one working academic (consciousness studies, philosophy of mind, cultural psychology)
   - Send: Module summary (1 page) + your best essay + brief request for comment
   - Archive response or non-response
   
5. **Comparative Assessment Analysis** (1,000 words):
   - Review all AI indicative grades and your self-assessments
   - Where did they converge? Diverge?
   - What pattern emerges?
   - What does this tell you about AI assessment capability?
   
6. **Decision:**
   - Proceed to Module 2?
   - Take a break and return?
   - Modify framework based on Module 1 experience?
   - Abandon and study differently?
